Hello.

I am a researcher in Applied Economics, with a career developed at the University of São Paulo (USP) and the State University of Ponta Grossa (UEPG), where I was part of research groups in development, innovation, and advanced data modeling. I was a member of GEPHAC - ESALQ/USP, actively participating in applied research projects and scientific production focused on agricultural productivity, rural credit, and regional economic development. During my PhD at USP, I developed studies on the regionalization of soybean productivity and livestock credit in Brazil, with results published in national and international journals. I also coordinated academic and university entrepreneurship initiatives as the founder and president of the Center for Entrepreneurial Education at UEPG.

My research focuses on the interface between applied economics, spatial econometrics, and data science, with an emphasis on advanced quantitative methods for evaluating public policies, regional development, and productive dynamics in the agricultural sector. I employ statistical, econometric, and machine learning techniques, integrating spatial analysis, georeferenced data, and satellite imagery to investigate complex socioeconomic issues. My work aims to generate contributions for both academic advancement and support for evidence-based public policymaking.
